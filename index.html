About CEEU

The Centre for Economic Empowerment Uganda (CEEU) is a non-governmental organization committed to enhancing the lives of vulnerable communities in Uganda through economic empowerment. With a mission to reduce poverty, CEEU focuses on health, education, and environmental protection, emphasizing sustainable development.

Established in 2018, CEEU operates in rural and underserved areas of Uganda, where access to quality services is limited. Our organization is registered with the Uganda Registration Services Bureau (URSB) and the NGO Bureau, with registration number 4114 and permit number 3893.

Our team of dedicated professionals works tirelessly to implement community-based programs that address the root causes of poverty and improve the overall well-being of marginalized populations.

Our Programs

CEEU focuses on several key areas to improve the lives of vulnerable communities in Uganda.

Maternal & Child Health

Our MNCH programs focus on improving maternal health education, healthcare access, and combating malnutrition. We've facilitated over 5,000 births in rural areas and educated 10,000 women on prenatal care.

Learn More

Education & Empowerment

We improve access to education by building schools, providing supplies, and offering vocational training. Our programs have benefited over 15,000 students, including 3,000 girls through scholarships.

Learn More

HIV/AIDS & TB Programs

Our HIV/AIDS initiatives focus on prevention, education, care, and treatment. We reach over 25,000 people annually with HIV prevention education and support 3,000 individuals through ART programs.

Learn More

Malaria Prevention

Our malaria programs emphasize prevention, early diagnosis, and effective treatment. We've distributed ITNs to 20,000 households and treated over 10,000 malaria cases, reducing malaria-related morbidity by 30%.

Learn More
